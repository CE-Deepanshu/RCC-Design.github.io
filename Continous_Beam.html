  • Refer to table No. 16 for clear cover as per exposure condition
  • Assume dia. of bar in tension, ø = ___ mm (generally near 20 mm)
  • Overall depth, D = d + c + ø/2 = ___ mm

Step 2: Effective Span

Refer to IS 456 Clause 22.2 (b):

  • If the width of the support is < 1/12 of the clear span, use Clause 22.2(a).
  • If support > 1/12 of clear span or 600 mm (whichever is less):
    • End span (one end fixed, other continuous) or intermediate spans: Effective span = clear span between supports.
    • End span (one end free, other continuous): Effective span = clear span + 0.5 × effective depth or clear span + 0.5 × width of discontinuous support, whichever is less.
    • Spans with roller/rocker bearings: Effective span = distance between centres of bearings.

Step 3: Calculation of Load

  • Dead load (DL)
    • Self weight of the beam = 25 × D / 1000 × b / 1000 × 1
    • + Superimposed dead load (if any)
    • Total factored dead load, Wu, DL = 1.5 × DL = KN/m
  • Live load (LL):
    Total live load = LL
    Total factored live load, Wu, LL = 1.5 × LL = KN/m

Read Clause 22.5: Moment and Shear Coefficients for Continuous Beams.

Step 4: Calculation of Moment & Shear Force

For beams with three or more spans (not differing by more than 15% of the longest), use coefficients from IS 456 Table 12 (Bending Moments) and Table 13 (Shear Forces):

Table 12: Bending Moment Coefficients (Clause 22.5.1)

Type of Load Near Middle of End Span At Middle of Interior Span At Support Next to End Support At Other Interior Supports
Dead load & imposed load (fixed) +1/12 +1/16 -1/10 -1/12
Imposed load (not fixed) +1/10 +1/12 -1/9 -1/9

To obtain the bending moment, multiply the coefficient by the total design load and effective span.

Shear Force Calculation

  • Vu = Coefficient.DL × (Wu, DL × l) + Coefficient.LL × (Wu, LL × l)

Table 13: Shear Force Coefficients (Clauses 22.5.1, 22.5.2)

Type of Load At End Support At Support Next to End Support (Outer) At Support Next to End Support (Inner) All Other Interior Supports
Dead load & imposed load (fixed) 0.4 0.6 0.55 0.5
Imposed load (not fixed) 0.45 0.6 0.6 0.6

For shear force, multiply the coefficient by the total design load.
