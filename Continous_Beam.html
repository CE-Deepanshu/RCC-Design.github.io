Design of Continuous Beam

Step 1: Depth of Beam

Clear span of beam = ___ mm

Effective depth of beam, d = span / 12

Assume clear cover of beam, c = ___ mm

Assume diameter of bar in tension, ø = ___ mm

Overall depth, D = d + c + ø/2

Step 2: Effective Span

Clause 22.2(b): Effective span depends on support width:

  • If support width < 1/12 of clear span → use Clause 22.2(a)
  • If support width > 1/12 of clear span or 600 mm (whichever is less):
    • End span (fixed-continuous) or intermediate → span = clear span
    • End span (free-continuous) → span = clear span + ½ × effective depth or ½ × width of discontinuous support (whichever is less)
      • Spans with roller/rocker bearings → span = distance between centres of bearings

Step 3: Load Calculation

Dead Load (DL):

Self weight = 25 × D/1000 + b/1000 × 1

+ Superimposed dead load (if any)

Wu_DL = 1.5 × DL

Live Load (LL):

Wu_LL = 1.5 × LL

Step 4: Moment & Shear Force

Moment:

Mu = Coeff_DL × (Wu_DL × l²) + Coeff_LL × (Wu_LL × l²)

Table 12: Bending Moment Coefficients

Shear Force:

Vu = Coeff_DL × (Wu_DL × l) + Coeff_LL × (Wu_LL × l)

Step 5: Depth Check

Mu = ___ kNm

Step 6: Steel Requirement

Ast_required = 0.5 × fck / fy × [1 − √(1 − 4.6 × Mu / fck × b × d²)] × b × d²
Ast_min = (0.85 × b × d) / fy
Area of 1-φ bar = (π × φ²) / 4
No. of bars = Ast_provided / Area of 1-φ bar

Step 7: Shear Check

τv = Vu / (b × d)

Check: τv > τcmax

Pt = (100 × Ast) / (b × d)
Tc = ___ N/mm²
τc_max = ___ N/mm²

Check: τc < τc_max

Check: τv < τc → If not, design for shear stress is required

For Vertical Stirrups (Clause 40.4(a))

Vus = Vu − τc × b × d
Asv = 2 × π × φ² / 4
Sv = 0.87 × fck × Asv × d / Vus

Max Stirrup Spacing (Clause 26.5.1.5)

  • Sv ≤ 0.75d
  • Sv ≤ 300 mm

Max spacing permitted = Minimum of above
