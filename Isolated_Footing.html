Design Steps for Square Isolated Footing

Steps 1 to 8 shown in simple boxes. (Font: Times New Roman)

1

Step 1 — Decide Footing Size

Take the column load (W). Assume the footing self-weight as 10% of W. Add them and apply a load factor of 1.5. Divide by ultimate soil bearing capacity to get footing area. For a square footing, side B = √area.

2

Step 2 — Net Upward Soil Pressure

Calculate net upward ultimate pressure using the factored column load and the footing area. Do not include the weight of the footing for this pressure calculation.

3

Step 3 — Find Footing Depth

Check bending moment at the face of the column and compute effective depth (d). Increase depth if needed for shear. Always provide at least 150 mm thickness.

4

Step 4 — Area of Steel

Use bending moment to find required steel area. Also ensure minimum steel is provided (as per code). Distribute reinforcement evenly in both directions.

5

Step 5 — One‑Way Shear Check

Check shear at a section d away from the column face. Calculate shear force and nominal shear stress and compare with concrete shear capacity. No shear bars are normally provided in isolated footings.

6

Step 6 — Two‑Way (Punching) Shear Check

Check punching shear around the column at d/2 from the periphery. Compute the critical perimeter and shear stress, then compare with the allowable punching shear capacity.

7

Step 7 — Development Length

Verify that bar embedment (development) length near the column face meets code requirements. Compare required development length with available embedment.

8

Step 8 — Summary & Sketch

Prepare a final summary with footing size, depth, steel details and draw a neat plan and section sketch for documentation and construction.
